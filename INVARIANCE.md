# Invariance

The theorems are structural. They follow from the form `c : X → X → B`
alone, so they hold of every self-classifying system: the reach is universal.

They are verified in one register: category theory, type theory, Lean,
English. That register is the coordinate system, not the fact. A structural
truth is coordinate-free: re-verifiable in any adequate register, invariant
under translation between them. This repo exhibits one verification. It does
not confine the truth to one language.

So "stated in this register" is not a limit on reach. The reach is universal;
the register is where the proof was written, replaceable by any other.

The one genuine incompleteness is Autology's account of itself. By
`no_complete_self_account`, every self-classifier has a hole in its
self-account, Autology included, in every register alike. This is not the
facts being local. It is complete self-account being impossible for every
self-classifier, this framework among them.

Grades, read correctly:

    ●●  a verified structural theorem: universal, register-independent,
        verified here. Not empirical, not language-local.
    ●   a reading: that some actual system has the structure. Defeasible.
        Whether a real system instantiates the form is separate from, and
        never certified by, the theorem about the form.
